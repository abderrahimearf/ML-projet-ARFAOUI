variables :

Relative Compactness: Indicates the relative compactness of the building, influencing the façade area and heat loss.

Wall Area: Indicates the area of the walls, which affects heat loss and heating needs.

Roof Area: Represents the area of the roof, which influences solar heat gain.

Overall Height: Indicates the total height of the building, an important factor for heat flow and natural lighting.

Orientation: Indicates the orientation of the building (e.g., north, south, east, west), which affects sun exposure and energy needs.

Glazing Area: Represents the area of windows, influencing heat gain and loss based on sun exposure.

Glazing Area Distribution: Indicates the distribution of glazed surfaces on the building, impacting natural light and thermal loads.

We are conducting an energy analysis using different building shapes simulated in Ecotect. The buildings differ in terms of window area, the distribution of that area, and orientation, among other parameters. The dataset consists of 8 features aimed at predicting two real responses: Heating Load (This is the amount of energy required to heat the building in winter) and Cooling Load (This is the amount of energy required to cool the building in summer).
